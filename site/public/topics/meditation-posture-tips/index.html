Tips for Meditation Posture

By Lisa K. February 5, 2025

After struggling with lower back pain during sits for almost two years, I’ve finally found a setup that works. Thought I’d share in case it helps others.

What helped me

  1. A higher cushion — I switched from a standard zafu to a taller one (about 6 inches). This tilted my pelvis forward enough that my spine could stack naturally.

  2. A folded blanket under my knees — For those of us who sit cross-legged, a little support under the knees takes pressure off the hips.

  3. The “puppet string” image — My teacher suggested imagining a string pulling from the crown of my head. Sounds simple, but it changed how I hold my upper body.

  4. Permission to use a chair — Seriously. Some of my best sits have been in a dining chair with my feet flat on the floor. There’s no medal for suffering through pain.

Resources

Our sangha library has a copy of “The Posture of Meditation” by Will Johnson. It’s a short read and very practical.

What works for you? I’d love to hear other tips, especially for those evening sits when the body is tired.
